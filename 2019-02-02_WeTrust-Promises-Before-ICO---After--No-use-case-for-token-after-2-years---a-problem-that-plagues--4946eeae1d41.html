WeTrust Promises Before ICO & After: No use case for token after 2 years — a problem that plagues cryptocurrency

Why should anyone buy TRST? This question arose multiple times during WeTrust’s ICO/Crowdsale. It can also still be seen now: why do you think people bought your token?

The team is dishonest and deceptive at best.

Before:

In a bitcointalk thread, prior to the ICO:

Q: Why is TRST token attractive for crowdsale participants?
There will only be 100M Trustcoins (TRST) in existence and no more will be created after the crowdsale. The coin will be essential for our trusted lending circles (aka, ROSCAs) to function (as all fees will be quoted in terms of Trustcoin). Future features that will be developed out from the trusted lending circles will also require Trustcoins as their "fuel"/ collateral. Does this help? Let me know if you have any questions - we're currently working on a nicely written blog post that goes into high detail about our coin to help the general audience.

Q: What profit sources exist for the TRST token holders?
Under advice from our lawyers, I cannot comment on how TRST tokens are a "profit source". That is for you to decide. However, I can provide you a piece I wrote on "crowdsale analysis". Smiley
https://docs.google.com/document/d/1bslchS9qtCQBzQvX2mV2yr18PppLXr7saT6x73-uMTk/edit?usp=sharing

From their own words — it is attractive because there are only 100M tokens (10M of which they have reserved for themselves).

Patrick also added his piece on crowdsale analysis, which basically says how to choose an ICO to invest in. It’s amusing to read it, as he highlights the importance of transparency (in the form of the code being open source, allowing him to verify the competency of the team), being able to meat goals, honesty of management, exchange adoption and others.

After:

Of course, nobody will outright say “buy our token and you’ll get rich” (with lawyers or without), but it is interesting to see that some of the arguments made in the ICO stage of the crypto project are never brought up again.

Now the team denies it is an investment. Question: TRST is a utility token, then why did Patrick Long, one of the founders mention that it is attractive to ICO participants because there are only 100M tokens? Why would I, a person who bought TRST to use it on the WeTrust platform care about it? Why did he post his piece on investment into ICOs? What place did it have there? Why was there no limit or warning for people buying a lot of these tokens? — it’s too much for fees.

I’m sure if you dig deeper you will find more occurrences of this.

Until this day TRST token has no utility. Do not be fooled by sentences like “It can be used in TLC” — yes, it can. But why would anyone do that? They can also use ETH, a much more stable cryptocurrency, which doesn’t have liquidity issues like TRST (Daily volume: $200), and is on virtually every cryptocurrency exchange out there. Why would you say this to us, the community? It’s an easy way out of the argument and it might just work for some people who don’t know much about the project or are not interested in the technical details (perhaps they bought it as an “investment”), but for the people that do know the details it just further distances the team from them.

What is TRST? A Paradox.

TRST is not an investment, it is not a donation, and it is not a utility token*. Then what is it?

Why do you think the people bought TRST token?

Who are the TRST ICO investors?

This doesn’t line up, by their own words TRST is not an investment, not a donation. By their actions it is not a utility. So, it is nothing?

The ICO participants are not investors and not donators. They certainly aren’t users either, as there is no usecase. Patrick calls them “supporters”.

It has been almost 2 years since the ICO. Of course, they could come up with any “usecase” for the token to justify it now, considering the nature of the team. Does it seem likely they’ll come forward and say “Yes, we messed up”? Or did they want to capitalize on the growing ICO craze?

* I have explained why TRST doesn’t actually have a utility above.

Conclusion

I do not see what the team is trying to achieve here. Let’s assume they are backpedaling now to avoid issues from SEC.

The problem is they also have (had) a community — their supporters, ICO investors. By lying to the community they pushed it away from the team. Indeed, if you look at the communication channels barely anyone is discussing anything.

People do not trust WeTrust anymore.

Nobody signed up to have a team having to deal with SEC, a lawsuit v a founder in addition to the general cryptocurrency-related problems that every other project faces. Many will abandon a project just upon hearing that. For the support of the community to remain during difficult times, there needs to be trust. If there is no trust there is no community left.

With the growing frustration of the token holders, some are considering a lawsuit against the team.

I also have a personal message for the team. I’m sure there are reading my posts and will continue to do so, as there is a lot that can be written about it and the posts will continue.

Dear WeTrust team,

People who bought TRST did not do so just to fund “your mission”. At best you could say they funded a usecase for the TRST token, which you still did not deliver. I only hope that you sometimes doubt and wonder “are we doing the right thing?”. People have told you this many times, I’m sure. If you are afraid of trouble from SEC, why are you not afraid of people writing complaints to them? Do you think you are right, and using an ICO to fund your project was the right thing to do? Again, do you not know the true reason why people bought your token? Patrick, you yourself are one of these people — you wrote an article about investing into ICOs for “handsome profit”. If the tables turned around and all of the TRST token holders were the WeTrust team and you were the investor, would you now be happy about the situation?

WeTrust Knew About Potential Legal Issues (with SEC)

In an interview to Forklog with George Li and Patrick Long of WeTrust it is revealed that the team knew of the potential legal issues (with SEC).

If you are following WeTrust Platform’s communication channels it is not foreign to now see, besides “financial inclusion” and “social good” another phrase: “legal issues”. Indeed, the team is facing a lawsuit with one of the founders — Ron Merom. The other legal issue, apparently, is regulation.

ForkLog: ICO, crowdsale, crypto fundraising and token sales altogether are all in so-called legal vacuum. Why won’t you consider venture capital financing?

WeTrust: We’ve followed a framework put out by Coinbase, and under guidance from our lawyers are calling our fundraising a Token Sale. In the US, the word ICO is too close to IPO which is heavily scrutinized by the SEC. That’s why I’m calling it a token sale. Anyway, we are working with Goodwin Proctor, 100+ year old technology and financial services law firm to ensure what we are doing complies with the law.

It is obvious that there is/was legal uncertainty regarding cryptocurrency. They already knew about SEC. WeTrust said they are working with a law firm to ensure what they are doing complies with the law.

And yet here we are now: essentially a token vacuum with no real usecase, no planned (or unplanned) exchange listings and no communication with investors.

The only people that lose on this are the ones that bought TRST tokens. Why is the team not doing anything for them? Are they moving to another more crypto-friendly country (their team is already international)? When will the investors know what is going on with their money? Bear market or bull, regulation or no regulation the team has already collected the money.
